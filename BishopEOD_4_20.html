How to reconcile them
The structure tells you what will happen mechanically if a catalyst fires. The internals tell you what institutions are doing right now. They answer different questions.
Positive internals without positive structure means the bid is real but the safety net is gone. If institutions are right and diplomacy holds — the magnets above ($715, $720, $725) pull price up. If they're wrong and the ceasefire fails tomorrow — there is no gamma cushion to absorb the move, and $700 accelerates it downward.
Key asymmetry: the upside path is slow and mechanical (grinding toward magnets). The downside path is fast and amplified (negative GEX below $700 accelerates). Reward is gradual. Risk is sudden.
Confirmed from a separate angle: positive gamma is present for the week overall, but requires selectivity. The broad momentum of last week does not carry over broadly — the free ride on the index is over.

Two weeks ago that Monday had $1.49B of gamma to cushion the move. Today's market has $175M. The internals signal may look identical — the mechanical environment underneath is not.

Charm + vanna — the silent forces still running
No escape hatch — both fire regardless of catalysts
Charm at +428K: dealers are still selling shares every single day from time decay alone. No catalyst needed. No escape hatch. It runs 24/7 in a call-heavy book — every day that passes, call deltas decay, dealers sell shares to rebalance.
Vanna still negative at −55K: call book dominates. Negative means the same IV move that was a tailwind when puts dominated is now a headwind. VIX is already up 10% today. If IV keeps expanding — dealers sell more.
Vanna flipped from tailwind to headwind because 87% of new post-rally flow was calls. The people who chased calls during the 13-day rally unknowingly flipped the switch against themselves.
What chop means right now
Sideways is not neutral in the current setup. Quiet day = IV compresses = vanna sells + charm sells = slow bleed from both directions simultaneously, with no gamma cushion to slow it.
The two escapes: (A) Massive rip higher — price surges, call deltas surge toward 1.0, dealer buying overwhelms charm selling. Needs a strong catalyst (ceasefire extension). (B) Controlled retracement — price pulls back, people buy puts, new put hedging offsets charm on calls, new gamma base forms.
Worst case: sideways chop — not moving up enough to overwhelm charm with delta buying, not moving down enough to trigger new put buying. Charm bleeds silently every day until something breaks.

Charm is the quiet assassin — no escape hatch, runs 24/7. Vanna is more dramatic but charm never stops. The combination means: even if nothing bad happens tomorrow, every day without a strong bullish catalyst is a day of structural erosion.
