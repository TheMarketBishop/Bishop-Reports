Week in Review — Narrative Arc

The week was the most volatile of the entire cycle by composite score, and yet SPX closed Friday at a new all-time high — a paradox that defines exactly the regime the market is in now.

Tuesday April 21 — the rip: The most destructive single session of the cycle by every measurable metric. −326.3M delta flow (all-time record). −$49.5B block net sell at 3.9× ratio. −$628M GEX (deepest negative reading of the cycle). A/D 0.58×, every exchange red. Every mega-cap liquidated simultaneously — NVDA −$7.43B (was top buyer for 3+ weeks), AAPL −$5.45B, SPY −$5.11B, QQQ −$4.98B. The lone buyer in the entire tape: AVGO +$3.22B. And then, 9 minutes after the close, Trump extended the ceasefire indefinitely. SPY ripped +0.4% in after-hours. The structural damage went into Wednesday with futures already covering ground.

Wednesday-Thursday — the chop: Wednesday morning composite ran +26.1 on the ceasefire extension, GEX healed back to +$496M. Then Thursday brought the most dramatic intraday session of the cycle — Tehran air defenses engaged drones around 1:34 PM, GEX collapsed to −$1.23B (deepest of cycle), SPY tagged $702.28, eight accelerators loaded below with zero magnets. The composite swung 25+ points four times in five sessions. The earnings tape was punishing every beat: TSLA −5%, IBM −8%, NOW −13%. Mega-cap concentration was being stress-tested in real time.

Friday April 24 — the reload: INTC blew out earnings (+27% pre-market), priced through its August 2000 dot-com all-time high. The Israel-Lebanon ceasefire was extended 3 weeks. The DOJ dropped the Powell criminal probe. NVDA hit $5T market cap. GOOGL crossed $4T after announcing +$40B more into Anthropic. The composite swung from −7.7 at the open to +22.7 by 2 PM. GEX flipped from −$184M to +$669M intraday — a $853M swing. And the institutional tape printed +$65B net buy at 3.5× — the largest single-day block accumulation in months, with NVDA alone +$11.96B across 3,506 prints with zero sell blocks. SPX closed at 7,160, a new all-time high, +13.3% off the March 30 low.

The thesis that held: Externally-triggered dips were buying opportunities again. Tuesday's institutional liquidation, Thursday's Tehran scare, and the multiple intraday flips through the week all resolved up — not because the geopolitical story improved (it didn't, materially), but because the structural bid kept reloading on every dip. This is the 5th confirmed cycle of the dip-buying thesis.

The pattern that broke: The "Iran noise filter" that held for 5+ sessions broke briefly Thursday on the Tehran air defense engagement (real escalation, not rhetoric). It then reasserted by Friday — the market filtered through Iran walking out of Pakistan and printed a new ATH anyway. The filter is intact, but its tolerance for actual kinetic events (vs. rhetoric) is unclear.

The pattern that strengthened: Mega-cap concentration. AI is now 45% of S&P market cap (record). NVDA at $5T, GOOGL at $4T, INTC at multi-decade highs. Asset managers added +$9.7B in Nasdaq futures in the week ending April 14 — largest weekly purchase in 10 years. Institutional positioning at +$39B is the highest since February. The thesis isn't broken; it's being defended at scale.

Bottom Line — Monday Setup

The structural carry into Monday is bullish — positive gamma, magnet stack at $715-$730, accelerators pushed below $700, vol crushed.
