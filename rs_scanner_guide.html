Overview

What This Is

This channel gets automated scanner messages every trading day. The scanner runs on a server and pulls data directly from the Schwab API — no manual work on my end once it's set up.

The whole point is to make sure the stocks I'm watching at 2 PM are actually the strongest names in the market. It doesn't tell you when to enter. It tells you what's worth looking at.

The RS rating is the same methodology IBD uses. It compares every stock's price performance against every other stock over multiple timeframes. A rating of 95 means that stock has outperformed 95% of everything else. That's who you want to be in.

The scanner is a filter, not a signal. It narrows down 900+ stocks to the handful worth pulling charts on. You still need to read the chart and find the setup yourself.

9:00 AM

Morning Briefing

This comes out every morning before the open. It's a quick scan of what's already moving and what my watchlist looks like coming into the day.

PREMARKET MOVERS Full Universe Scan

Stocks from the full liquid universe that are already moving 2% or more before the bell. These are worth watching regardless of whether they're on my existing watchlist. The filter requires liquid options and a price above $10 so you're not looking at garbage.

If something shows up here with a strong ST RS and decent relative volume, it goes on my radar for the day.

MOVING Bench Names Already In Motion

My established watchlist names that are already moving 2%+ premarket. If something from my bench is gapping up 3-4% before the open, that goes to the top of my list for the day. These are the setups I'm most focused on.

QUIET Bench Names Sitting Still

The rest of the bench. Still worth pulling up at 2 PM, just not as urgent as the names already moving. These are still market leaders — they just don't have a catalyst yet today.

9:45 / 10:00 / 10:15 AM

Daytrade Scans

Three scans in the first 45 minutes after the open. The reason there are three instead of one is that the first 15 minutes are chaotic. By 9:45 you start to see who's actually holding. By 10:15 the picture is clearer and the fakeouts have mostly shaken out.

CALLS Strongest Intraday Leaders

The top 8 stocks leading the market right now. Ranked by how strong they are today versus all other stocks, filtered to only include names that are also strong this week. These are the stocks you'd be looking at for call setups.

PUTS Weakest Intraday Stocks

The 8 weakest stocks right now. Ranked by how weak they are today versus all other stocks. These are the stocks breaking down that you'd look at for put setups.

Same rule applies here. The scanner shows you who's strong and who's weak. You still need to pull the chart and find a clean setup before entering anything.

3:00 PM

Intraday Scan

This is the same full scanner that runs at the close but it runs while the market is still open. The reason this one matters more than the closing scan for same-day trading is timing.

The morning briefing shows you yesterday's RS data. By the time the closing scan posts at 4:33 PM, the market is already closed. The 3 PM scan catches names that have been moving all day and now show elevated RS going into the last hour — and you can still act on it.

This is what I'm primarily looking at when I sit down at 2 PM to pick my setups for the 3:55 PM entry.

HEATING UP Early Ignition

Stocks where short-term RS is accelerating significantly above long-term RS. This is the signal I watch most closely. My biggest swing runner setups started by showing up here a day or two before the main move.

ACTIVE BENCH Established Leaders

Stocks with both long-term and short-term RS above 80. These are the confirmed market leaders. If one of these sets up cleanly at 2 PM, it's the highest probability play available.

UNUSUAL OPTIONS Abnormal Flow

Stocks with abnormal options activity relative to their normal baseline. Filtered by volume-to-open-interest ratio so it's not just showing you the same mega caps every day. When a mid-cap shows 3-4x its normal open interest in a single session, something is going on worth paying attention to.

4:33 PM

Closing Scan

The main nightly scan. Full picture after the close using final closing data. This is what sets up tomorrow's watchlist.

Everything you see here — the bench, the heating up list, the unusual activity — this is what I'm reviewing in the evening to know what names I'm watching tomorrow.

Heating Up

ST RS accelerating above LT RS. These are the early-stage setups before the move is obvious to everyone. This is where Swing Runner setups start.

Active Bench

LT RS and ST RS both above 80. Confirmed leaders. These are the names on my charts every single day.

Column Definitions

Every column across all three scanner sections.

Column What It Shows
LT Long-term RS rating. 252 trading days using IBD's weighted formula. Measures sustained momentum over the past year.
ST Short-term RS rating. 20 trading days. Measures recent momentum over the past month.
Gap ST minus LT. Shows how fast momentum is accelerating. A high Gap number means the stock is moving faster recently than its long-term trend — early ignition signal.
RVol Relative volume. Today's volume divided by 20-day average. 2.0x means double the normal volume. Confirms institutional participation.
52WkH% Percent below the 52-week high. A stock at -5% is near its highs. Anything close to 0% with high RS is in a very strong position.
Day% Daily percentage change from previous close.
Liq Options liquidity grade. Three dots — amber = filled, dark = empty. Three filled = Exceptional liquidity. Two = Solid. One = thinner but still tradeable.
NEW This stock wasn't on the bench 48 hours ago. It just broke through the RS threshold. Worth paying extra attention to — fresh momentum.
Vol/OI Options volume divided by open interest. A ratio above 1.0x means more contracts traded today than the total existing open interest — genuinely unusual. Above 2.0x shows in amber.
C:P Ratio Calls to puts ratio. 3:1 means 3 call contracts for every put contract traded. Shows overall directional bias in the options market for that stock.
Intraday RS Daytrade scan only. Today's move from open, ranked against all other stocks right now. 98 means stronger than 98% of everything else today.
Weekly RS Daytrade scan only. Last 5 trading days performance, ranked against all other stocks. Confirms whether today's strength is part of a trend or an isolated move.

My Daily Workflow

How I actually use these scans from start to finish every trading day.

9:00 AM — Glance at the morning briefing. Note anything in the premarket movers section. If a bench name is already up 3%+, that's going to the top of my list today.
9:45 to 10:15 AM — Check the daytrade scans if I'm watching the open. Look for call and put candidates with clean setups. The 10:15 scan is usually the most reliable read.
2:00 PM — Open charts on the Heating Up names and Active Bench from the 3 PM scan. I'm not scanning anything from scratch. I already know exactly which names are the strongest in the market.
3:55 PM — Enter whatever setup confirmed during the 2:00 to 3:55 PM window. This is the entry window for swing trades.
Evening — Review the 4:33 PM closing scan. Check who's on the bench, who's Heating Up, and whether anything new showed up in unusual activity. Takes 2-3 minutes. Done for the day.

Important Notes

This is not trade advice. The scanner shows you what's strong. It does not tell you to buy or sell anything. Every entry decision is yours. Read the chart. Find the setup. Manage your risk.

Weekend and after-hours data is thin. If you see low RS numbers or n/a values on a weekend, that's normal. The scanner needs live market data to produce meaningful rankings. During market hours on trading days, the data is accurate.

The bench changes every day. A stock can be on the bench one day and off the next if RS drops. The NEW flag tells you when something just entered — these are worth extra attention because fresh momentum is often the strongest momentum.
