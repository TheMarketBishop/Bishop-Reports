Quick Reference — Key Terms 🟡

Brief definitions of the technical terms in this report. Skip if fluent.

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Aggregate dealer gamma. Positive = dealers stabilize price. Negative = dealers amplify moves in both directions.
GEX Flip
Price level where net GEX crosses zero. Above = cushion. Below = trapdoor. Today it's $7,070.
Dealer Short Delta
Aggregate short-stock hedge dealers carry. Shrinking means their forced-buying engine is fading.
Accelerator
A strike with heavy negative gamma — breaking through forces dealer selling that amplifies the move.
Magnet
A strike with heavy positive gamma — dealers hedge around it, pulling price back to it.
Charm
Delta decay from time passing. In a call-heavy book, charm bleeds support daily regardless of price.
Vanna
How dealer delta moves with IV. Positive vanna = vol crush triggers dealer buying.
NCP / NPP
Net Call / Put Premium. Dollar-weighted flow into each side. Today: puts dominate 10:1.
A/D Ratio
Advancing stocks ÷ declining. Above 1.0 = broad participation up. Today 0.58x — broad participation down.
P/C Ratio
Puts ÷ calls. Above 1.0 = heavy hedging/bearish. 2.51 today is an extreme reading.
IV Rank
Where IV sits in its 52w range (0–1). Today: 0.274 for SPX — not extreme, room to expand.
Dark Pool Level
Price where institutions traded significant size off-exchange. Today's key: $706.18 on SPY.

The News-vs-Noise Problem 🟡
Overnight news matters. And also doesn't.
Every headline that hits between now and the 9:30 AM open tomorrow can reverse the setup entirely. The structure is balanced on a 25-hour binary where a single statement from Tehran or Washington rewrites the chart.
The Vance trip went from "on" to "paused" to "uncertain" to "could resume quickly" — all within four hours today. Iran said it won't talk until the blockade is lifted. Then Iran's Fatemeh Mohajerani said their delegation is led by a field commander "with military and diplomatic experience" without confirming whether talks are actually underway in Pakistan. The headlines contradict themselves hour by hour.
This is the core trap: overnight news feels decisive because it's the most recent input, but most of it gets reversed or reinterpreted by morning. The first version of a breaking story is usually wrong. Reports of troop movements, leaders' statements, and "paused" vs "on hold" framing are noise in aggregate — the signal is only the final status at 9:30 AM tomorrow.
What is actually signal
Ceasefire expires Wed 4:50 PM ET. That is a hard clock. Not a headline, a deadline.
Brent above $100. Markets are pricing through oil regardless of Twitter noise. Oil is running ahead of stocks as the purest real-time read on Hormuz.
Trump: "I have all the time in the world." A direct, public statement of leverage, not a rumor. It matters more than any reported diplomatic back-and-forth.
Prediction markets on Hormuz normalization by Apr 30: 20¢ yes / 79¢ no. Money-weighted probability, not opinion.
What is mostly noise
"C-17s moving to the Middle East" — high-engagement, visually dramatic, ambiguous meaning. Could be staging for kinetic action; could be routine positioning to pressure negotiations. Cannot distinguish from a screenshot.
"Vance trip paused / could resume" — process headlines, not outcome headlines. The only data that matters is whether Iran sends a delegation before 4:50 PM Wednesday. Everything before that is theater.
Presidential speech on non-market topics (Tim Cook commentary, NATO criticism, Fed rates) — landed pre-market but had no durable price impact. Markets don't know what to do with these on binary days and mostly ignore them within hours.

Framework: only three news items actually move the structure overnight. (1) Iran confirms a delegation to Islamabad. (2) Ceasefire is extended by either side. (3) Kinetic action begins before the deadline. Everything else is process noise that shifts sentiment by hours but not days. Trade the structure in front of you; treat overnight news as potential-but-not-likely to flip it.

The honest caveat on overnight news
Everything written above can be reversed by a single headline in the next 16 hours. That is not a weakness of the analysis — that is the actual state of the market right now. The structure is not predictive; it's conditional on outcomes that have not happened yet. Treat any bold directional conviction tonight as overconfident.

The broader frame says that whatever happens tomorrow, the S&P will likely be higher 30 days later than it is right now in almost every historical comparison.
